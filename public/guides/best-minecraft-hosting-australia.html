Ranked list

  1. Shockbyte — Beginner-friendly Minecraft hosting. Shockbyte is a simple pick for new server owners who want common Minecraft features without spending too much.
  2. PebbleHost — Lowest-cost private servers. PebbleHost fits players who want cheap RAM-based plans and already know roughly how much memory their server needs.
  3. Apex Hosting — Support-focused setup. Apex Hosting costs more than budget hosts, but it makes sense if you want easier setup, support and a more guided Minecraft experience.

How to choose

Start with player location, expected online player count, modpack size, backup needs and whether you want support to help with setup. Do not pick only by the cheapest first-month price.
