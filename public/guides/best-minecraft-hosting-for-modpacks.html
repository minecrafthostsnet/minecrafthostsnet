Ranked list

  1. BisectHosting — Modpacks and balanced value. BisectHosting is the clean middle ground: stronger modpack support than bare budget hosts without jumping straight to premium pricing.
  2. Apex Hosting — Support-focused setup. Apex Hosting costs more than budget hosts, but it makes sense if you want easier setup, support and a more guided Minecraft experience.
  3. Nodecraft — Polished panel and multi-game hosting. Nodecraft is not the cheapest Minecraft-only host, but it has a polished panel, backups and useful tools for people who swap between games.
  4. PebbleHost — Lowest-cost private servers. PebbleHost fits players who want cheap RAM-based plans and already know roughly how much memory their server needs.
  5. Shockbyte — Beginner-friendly Minecraft hosting. Shockbyte is a simple pick for new server owners who want common Minecraft features without spending too much.

How to choose

Start with player location, expected online player count, modpack size, backup needs and whether you want support to help with setup. Do not pick only by the cheapest first-month price.
